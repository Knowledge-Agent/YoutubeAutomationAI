Niche choice is the highest-leverage decision in YouTube Automation. A strong workflow cannot fully compensate for a weak niche.

## CPM basics

CPM (Cost Per Mille) represents the earnings per 1,000 views. Higher CPM niches earn more money per view.

### CPM ranges by category

- Finance / Business: **$15–50**
- Legal: **$12–30**
- Health: **$8–15**
- Tech: **$6–12**
- Entertainment: **$3–8**
- Education: **$5–15**

## Top niches to evaluate

### 1. Finance and investing

- **CPM:** $15–50
- **Competition:** Medium-High
- **Growth potential:** High

**Sub-niches:**
- Stock market analysis
- Cryptocurrency education
- Personal finance tips
- Real estate investing
- Tax strategies

**Why it works:**
- High advertiser value
- Consistent search interest
- Loyal audience base
- Multiple monetization options

**Challenges:**
- Requires accuracy and disclaimers
- Regulatory considerations
- Saturated in some areas

## Niche evaluation framework

### Questions to ask

1. **Market demand**
   - Is there search interest?
   - Are people actively looking for this content?

2. **Competition analysis**
   - Who are the top creators?
   - Can you differentiate?

3. **Content availability**
   - Can you source enough material?
   - Is it sustainable long-term?

4. **Monetization potential**
   - What’s the CPM range?
   - Are there other revenue streams?

5. **Personal fit**
   - Do you have interest?
   - Do you have enough domain understanding?

## 2026 emerging opportunities

- AI productivity workflows

## Selection checklist

### Must-have

- Proven demand
- Sustainable content pipeline
- Viable CPM and monetization
- Long-term publishing fit

### Nice-to-have

- Multiple subniches
- Evergreen potential
- Lower creator concentration
- More than one monetization angle

### Red flags

- Highly saturated angle with no differentiation
- Legal/compliance exposure you cannot handle
- Niche requiring expertise you do not possess
- Limited source material

Choose a niche you can publish in weekly for 12 months—consistency compounds faster than perfect positioning.
